Filipino Cuisine

Many kinds of dishes are made here in the Philippines. Since there are thousands of islands, each part of the Philippines adds its spin on certain dishes because of the availability of local ingredients. Filipino cuisine is much more different from the cuisines of other countries that surround the Philippines. This is due to the long history of the archipelago nation being colonized by many countries in the past. Many of the dishes in the Philippines have roots that stretch to countries like Taiwan, China, Spain, and even India.


Adobo

This is adobo, which is considered to be the Philippine's national dish. Adobo is a dish that perfectly balances both bitter and savory tastes together to create a tasty experience like no other. Adobo gets its taste from the use of both vinegar and soy sauce in its making. The 1:1 balance of the two ingredients helps to create the unique taste of Adobo. Adobo can be served with many kinds of meats such as pork, chicken, eggs, and even fish. It is always served with rice like many other Filipino dishes.


Halo-halo

This is halo-halo. This is a dessert that is very popular amongst the Filipino population and tourists that visit the country. Halo-halo is a colorful ice cream dessert that is served with ube ice cream (ube is a kind of purple yam), many kinds of jellies, condensed milk, crushed ice, corn, flan, and cornflakes. The word halo-halo in English means "mix-mix" when translated from Tagalog, the Philippine language. When you are served halo-halo, all the ingredients are stacked on top of one another in either a bowl or a tall glass. You are then supposed to mix all of the ingredients with a spoon until they become homogenous.
